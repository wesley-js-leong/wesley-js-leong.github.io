Hi! I'm Wesley Leong (梁家轩). 

My pronouns are he/him.

I am interested in topics at the intersection of neuroscience, computation, and language. In Fall 2021, I will start as a PhD student in the University of Connecticut's Psychological Sciences program, focusing on Language and Cognition.

Previously, I was a research engineer at Halo Neuroscience in San Francisco. There, I used a combination of behavioral and neuroimaging methods to study the effects of transcranial electrical stimulation on human performance. I also wrote Python code for research uses such as experiment presentation, data analysis, and deep learning.

Before that, I graduated from New York University with a BS in Neuroscience. My thesis was on cerebellar time perception, which I wrote while in Eric Lang's Lab. I also did some work in linguistics, including an MEG study in the Neurolinguistics Lab and a funded project on tense variation in Singaporean English.

On a related note, I am from the tiny island nation of Singapore.
